Taylor Swift

Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. An influential figure in popular culture, she is known for her songwriting, musical versatility, artistic reinventions, and influence on the music industry. She is a leading music artist of the 21st century.


Swift signed with Big Machine Records in 2005 and debuted as a country singer with her albums Taylor Swift (2006) and Fearless (2008). The singles "Teardrops on My Guitar", "Love Story", and "You Belong with Me" found crossover success on country and pop radio formats. Speak Now (2010) expanded her country pop sound with rock influences, while Red (2012) explored electronic dynamics. Swift recalibrated her artistic identity from country to pop with the synth-pop release 1989 (2014), while enduring media scrutiny updates throughout the urban-infused Reputation (2017).


Shifting to Republic Records in 2018, she released the eclectic pop album Lover (2019) and re-recorded four of her first six albums due to a dispute with Big Machine. She explored indie-folk on the 2020 albums Folklore and Evermore, synth-pop on Midnights (2022), and folk-rock on The Tortured Poets Department (2024). The Eras Tour (2023–2024) and its associated concert film became the highest-grossing concert tour of all time.
