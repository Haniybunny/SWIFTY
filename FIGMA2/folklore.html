Folklore (stylized in all lowercase) is the eighth studio album by the American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift. It was surprise-released on July 24, 2020, through Republic Records. Conceived during quarantine in early 2020 amidst the COVID-19 pandemic, the album explores deep themes of escapism, nostalgia, and romanticism. Swift recorded her vocals in a built-in home studio in Los Angeles while collaborating virtually with producers Aaron Dessner and Jack Antonoff from their respective studios across the country.

Using a set of characters and story arcs to depict fictional narratives, the album departs from the strictly autobiographical songwriting that had characterized Swift's past albums. Instead, it presents what she called "a collection of songs and stories that flowed like a stream of consciousness" born directly out of her isolation. Experimenting with new musical styles, Folklore consists of mellow ballads driven by piano, strings, orchestration, and muted percussion. Music critics classified the genre into a stripped-down mixture of folk, pop, indie rock, and electro-folk.

The album's title was inspired by the lasting legacy of folktales, and its visual aesthetic famously adopts an atmospheric cottagecore theme. It represents a massive sonic pivot from the upbeat electropop tones of its predecessor, Lover, moving toward a cohesive, relaxed, and poetic atmosphere. The release was accompanied by the highly praised concert documentary Folklore: The Long Pond Studio Sessions, which featured Swift's first live performances and creative commentary regarding the project's development.
